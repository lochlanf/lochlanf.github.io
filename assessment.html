1.1 Assessment Brief

The 1000G project was a watershed moment in clinical genomics. These data provided critical insights into the normal variation that occurs within the human genome and has informed pathogenicity predictions in clinical practice. This project was made possible by the participation of individuals and the donation of biospecimens for DNA sequencing analysis. The sequencing analysis performed on these samples was multimodal and included whole exome sequencing. Sequences were then analysed using a standardised bioinformatic pipeline to identify patient specific and population variants. These data provided insights into the population frequency of common variants. In this assessment, you will perform a re- analysis of sequencing data from a 1000G patient. You will apply the skills and techniques that have acquired in weeks 1 through 4 to call genomic variants from short-read whole exome sequencing.

You will be provided a set of sequencing reads and perform an assessment of sequence quality, conducting read trimming, mapping the reads to the human reference genome and calling variants. You will submit a single, end-to-end script that you used in your analysis, and prepare a short video presentation describing your results at each stage. There will be a 2-3 minute in-person Q&A session thereafter.
